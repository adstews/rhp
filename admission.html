Admissions

Everything you need to know about joining our structured sober living community in Madison, CT.

Who We Accept

Is Right Path Right for You?

✓ Good Candidates

  • ✓ Individuals completing rehab or a residential treatment program
  • ✓ Those who can live independently in a community setting
  • ✓ People willing to attend daily 12-step meetings
  • ✓ Those with co-occurring PTSD, ADHD, depression, or anxiety

📋 Admission Steps

  • 1 Initial phone call or form submission
  • 2 Interview with staff and current house members
  • 3 Your clinician helps set up your clinical plan
  • 4 Move in and begin your recovery journey
What's Included

More Than a Place to Stay

  • ✓ Private rooms in gender-specific homes
  • ✓ Family-style dinners with housemates
  • ✓ Free gym membership at Coastal Fitness (sauna, yoga, Pilates, barre)
  • ✓ Clinical partnerships including neurofeedback and EMDR
  • ✓ Individualized "wrap-around" clinical support
  • ✓ Access to a thriving local sober community

Program Structure

Recovery Requirements

  • ✓ 90 meetings in 90 days, then 5 per week
  • ✓ Work with an experienced therapist and 12-step sponsor
  • ✓ 30–40 hour weekly commitment (job, school, or volunteer)
  • ✓ Random drug testing and breathalyzer compliance
  • ✓ Attend weekly house meetings
  • ✓ Maintain your living space and share in household chores

🕑 Recommended Length of Stay

Research from SAMHSA shows the most effective length of stay for sustainable recovery is approximately 14 months. Right Path supports long-term stays to give you the best foundation.

FAQ

Common Questions

What should I bring?

Clothes for one week (work and workout clothes), laundry supplies, your resume, sports equipment or a bike if you'd like, and a week's worth of breakfast and lunch groceries. Dinner is provided family-style by housemates on a rotating schedule.

Can I have visitors?

Visitors are generally not permitted in the house to maintain a safe recovery environment. You're welcome to meet friends and family in public places.

Is there a curfew?

Yes. Curfews help create structure and safety. Most curfews are set between 8–10 PM, with extra flexibility on weekends.

Do you accept insurance?

Right Path House is a private pay program. We accept AutoPay, Venmo, personal checks, cash, and credit cards (3.5% convenience charge on credit card payments).
