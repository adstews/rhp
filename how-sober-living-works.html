How Sober Living Works

A look at daily life, house structure, and what to expect at Right Path House.

Daily Life

A Day at Right Path

Every day is built around purpose, connection, and recovery.

Morning

Morning meetings for those not at work or IOP. Start your day with intention and connection.

Daytime

Work, school, or volunteer commitment (30–40 hours/week). Therapy sessions, gym time, or wellness activities.

Evening

Family-style shared dinner with housemates. 12-step meetings, workshops, or in-house programming.

Night

Curfew provides structure and safety. Time for journaling, reading, or connecting with your sponsor.

Structure

House Expectations

Clear rules create a safe, predictable environment where recovery can thrive.

🚫

Zero Tolerance

Complete abstinence from drugs and alcohol. Regular random drug testing and breathalyzers.

📚

Meeting Attendance

90 meetings in 90 days initially, then 5 per week. Plus 8–10 hours of in-house workshops.

💪

Work Requirement

30–40 hours per week of purposeful activity — job, school, or volunteer work.

🧠

Therapy Required

All residents must be engaged with a therapist and working with a 12-step sponsor.

🏡

House Responsibilities

Keep your space clean, share in chores, and attend weekly house meetings.

🕐

Curfew

Evening curfews between 8–10 PM with flexibility on weekends. Creates routine and safety.

Where We Are

Life on the Connecticut Shore

Our homes are located in the coastal towns of Madison and Clinton, CT.

More Than a House — A Community

Our homes are walkable to the beach, downtown shops, 12-step meetings, and public transit. The area has a thriving sober community with strong AA and NA networks. Residents enjoy bikes, kayaks, and a free gym membership to explore the beautiful shoreline area.
